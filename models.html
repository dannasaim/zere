BMW 1 M COUPÉ (E82)



In December 2012, BMW officially introduced the “charged” two-door BMW 1-series M Coupe (E82), the world debut of which took place in January at the 2011 Detroit Auto Show.

BMW M2 (F87, G87)


The market launch of the second generation BMW M2 is scheduled for 2022, the 50th anniversary year of BMW M GmbH. With its unmistakable M styling, the updated model symbolizes the brand's commitment to motorsport.

BMW M3 (E30/2S, E46/2S)

Initially, the BMW M3 (E30/2S, E30/2SC) was conceived as a road version of a racing car. However, the interest in it was so enormous that in total much more than 5,000 cars were sold - the volume required for Group A approval.

BMW M4 (F82, F83, G82)


Created from the ground up, this car is significantly different from the BMW 3 Series due to the rear-set interior, longer bonnet and shortened overhangs - all of which create a more expressive and striking appearance.

BMW M5 (E28S, E34S, E34/5S, E39S, E60, E61, F10, F90)



Visually, the first BMW M5 (E28S) of 1985 was almost no different from a standard sedan. But the hand-built cars were equipped with an updated engine from the BMW M1 and could therefore reach speeds of more than 250 km/h.

BMW M6 (E63, E64, F06, F12, F13)


The M6's predecessor, the BMW M635CSi, began rolling off the production line in 1984. This coupe, created by Paul Braque, was equipped with a reworked engine from the BMW M1 with 286 hp. With. (210 kW) and became one of the fastest sports cars of its time.
